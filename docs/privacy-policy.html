Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 7, 2026

Spacecraft AR Track ("the App") is an augmented-reality app that overlays the positions of spacecraft, the Moon, and the Sun on your camera feed. This policy explains what data the App accesses and how it is used.

1. Data We Access

The App uses the following device capabilities:

  • Camera — Used solely to display the live camera feed as a background for the AR overlay. No images or video are recorded or stored.
  • Location — Your GPS coordinates are used on-device to calculate the positions of celestial objects relative to your location. Coordinates are also sent to the NASA JPL Horizons API to compute spacecraft positions (see Section 2).
  • Sensors — Accelerometer, gyroscope, and magnetometer data is used on-device only to determine your phone's orientation.

2. Network Requests

The App makes requests to one external service:

  • NASA JPL Horizons API (ssd.jpl.nasa.gov) — Your approximate location coordinates and the current time are sent to NASA's Horizons system to retrieve spacecraft ephemeris data. NASA's Horizons API is a public, free service. See NASA JPL Horizons for details.

No other network requests are made. There is no analytics, advertising, or tracking of any kind.

3. Data Storage

The App does not store any personal data. It does not have a database, user accounts, or persistent logs. All calculations happen in memory and are discarded when the App is closed.

4. Data Sharing

The App does not sell, rent, or share your data with any third parties. Your location is sent only to NASA JPL Horizons as described above.

5. Children's Privacy

The App is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date.
